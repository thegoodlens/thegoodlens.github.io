Seven mornings

A week of the same walk, the same hour, and what showed up differently each day — noted one photograph at a time.

the same walk, the same hour, for a week. i didn't go looking for anything in particular — only wanted to see what showed up differently each day, if I paid attention.

the mist hadn't lifted yet. neither had I, if I'm honest — this one was more duty than devotion.

she does this every day, rain or not. nobody photographs her for it. I think that's exactly why it counts.

the smoke caught the light before the light reached me. grace arrives first where you're not looking.

no reason for the laughing. that's the part I keep trying to relearn.

the last morning, I almost didn't bring the camera. this was the one I'm most glad I kept.

seven days of the same walk taught me the same thing five different ways: it was never about finding something new. it was about staying long enough to actually see what was already there.
