The weight a flower leaves behind

I found it on the path this morning, already crushed under someone's step. And yet, bending close, the fragrance was still there.

I found it on the path this morning, already crushed under someone's step. And yet, bending close, the fragrance was still there — the same as before, as if it hadn't noticed what had happened to it.

My Guru once said that grace does not ask to be noticed. It simply continues. I think this is what he meant — that the flower was never trying to be beautiful for me. It was only being what it was, and that was enough to leave something behind.

even the wounded flower does not withhold its scent.

I want to become like that — soft enough to be stepped on, and still offering something good to whoever passes by.
